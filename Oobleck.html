Oobleck: Non-Newtonian Fluid

Oobleck with Cornstarch Powder:

Isn't it fascinating that when you hit Oobleck hard, it becomes as solid as a rock, but when you touch it gently, it flows like a soft liquid? Even more intriguing, when you pass sound waves through it, Oobleck dances to the rhythm, reacting differently to each sound.

Non-Newtonian Fluid Definition

A fluid that does not follow Newton's law of viscosity, meaning its viscosity can change under stress.

Examples: Ketchup, toothpaste, and Oobleck.

Uses in Real Life

Body Armor: Hardens upon impact, providing protection.

Vibration Damping: Absorbs and dissipates energy in machinery.

Earthquake Protection: Absorbs shock waves, reducing building damage.

Procedure

How to Make Oobleck

Ingredients: 1 cup of cornstarch, 1/2 cup of water.

Instructions:

  • Slowly mix water into the cornstarch.
  • Adjust the consistency until it feels like thick pancake batter.
  • Optional: Add food coloring for a fun twist!

Demonstration: Show its properties by applying stress (e.g., hitting or squeezing it).
